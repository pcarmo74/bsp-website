The problem you're solving

Every site assessment
starts the same way.

The first 20 minutes of every homeowner conversation is the same — permits, timelines, costs, complexity. You answer these questions manually, on every call, before you've seen the space. That's your expertise being given away for free.

01
Homeowners don't understand permits
They land on your website not knowing if they need 2 permits or 7, how long it takes, or whether their legal suite qualifies for the $10K incentive. That uncertainty stops them from calling. Or they call unprepared and waste both your time.
02
Your website is a brochure, not a tool
It shows your work. It lists your services. But it doesn't do anything for a visitor who's on the fence. A homeowner who leaves your site without calling is a lost lead — and you never knew they were there.
03
Cold site assessments waste everyone's time
You drive to a property not knowing if it's a $30K family basement or a $150K legal suite with a full permit package. The first 20 minutes of the visit are qualification work you could have done automatically before you left.
04
Your competitors have the same contact form
In 2026 every contractor has a website with a contact form. None of them have an interactive tool that educates homeowners, pre-qualifies their project, and delivers a complete brief to your inbox automatically. Until now.
How it works

From website visitor
to briefed lead.

The tool runs automatically. You get the lead brief. You make the call knowing exactly what you're walking into.

01
Homeowner finds your website
They see a free tool — "Check Your Permit Requirements." No email required to start. They're curious. They click.
02
They answer 6 questions
Project purpose, plumbing scope, entrance requirements, budget range, whether they have drawings, and their timeline. Takes 3 minutes. No technical knowledge required.
03
They get instant results
Permit complexity score (Low/Moderate/High), exact number of permits required, realistic City of Calgary processing timeline, and whether they qualify for the $10K Secondary Suite Incentive.
04
You get their full brief instantly
The moment they submit — their name, email, phone, and complete project scope arrives in your inbox. You call them knowing their project type, complexity, budget range, and timeline expectations. The first conversation starts differently.
